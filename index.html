About Us

We're a group of crochet enthusiasts based in Sacramento. We're a passionate bunch of yarn lovers who've come together to share, learn, and spread the joy of crochet. Whether you're a seasoned pro or just picked up your first hook, there's a place for you here. Whether you're a newbie or a pro, join us for monthly meet-ups, workshops, and crochet-alongs! Monthly Meet-ups: Held at local cafes and parks, our meet-ups are the heart and soul of our group. It's where we stitch, chat, and sip on our favorite brews. Workshops: For those keen to level up their skills, we host occasional workshops taught by members or invited experts. From intricate patterns to mastering basics, we cover it all. Charity Drives: We believe in giving back. Every year, we host charity drives where we craft blankets, hats, and scarves for those in need in our community.

Crafting for a Cause

Crochet, an age-old craft, has evolved from a quiet pastime into a vibrant community activity. With the rise of local craft fairs, online marketplaces, and social media platforms, handmade crochet items are gaining popularity for their uniqueness and the craftsmanship they embody. But beyond the aesthetics and the joy of creation, there's a deeper layer to the crochet community – a stitch of compassion, of giving back. There are countless talented individuals who wield hooks and yarn, creating intricate patterns that manifest into beautiful pieces. From cozy blankets and beanies to elegant shawls and plush toys, these crafted items often carry a personal touch that store-bought ones simply can't replicate. Yet, amidst this creativity, many artisans choose to transform their skill into a noble endeavor – dedicating a portion or even all of their sales to charity. Why? Because they recognize the potential their craft holds: to not just create, but to also make a difference.

The Impact

Support for Causes: Whether it's contributing to children's hospitals, aiding disaster relief efforts, or supporting local shelters, funds raised from crochet sales can provide much-needed financial assistance to various charitable causes. Promoting Awareness: Often, crochet artisans partner with specific charities or causes, which in turn brings attention to issues that may otherwise go unnoticed. For instance, crafting pink scarves during Breast Cancer Awareness Month or blue hats for anti-bullying campaigns can spark conversations and spread knowledge. Empowering Artisans: Beyond the monetary contributions, such initiatives empower creators by adding a purpose-driven dimension to their work. It provides a sense of fulfillment that transcends commercial success. Community Building: Charity-driven crochet projects often bring together communities. Collaborative projects, such as creating a large quilt for a children's home or organizing charity crochet-a-thons, foster connections and strengthen community bonds.
